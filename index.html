About Me

Hi, I am Divya Rawat. I was born and raised in the Delhi NCR region with my family roots nestled in the serene Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, India. My curiosity for the fundamental mysteries of nature blossomed during my school years into a profound passion for physics. I pursued this passion by completing a BSc in Physics at the University of Delhi (2011–2014) and an MSc at Banaras Hindu University (2014–2016), where my fascination deepened for Physics. Driven to understand the complex behavior of matter under extreme conditions, I earned my PhD at the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur (2016–2021), focusing on accretion processes in compact objects. Following my doctorate, I contributed as a postdoctoral researcher at the AstroSat Science Support Cell at IUCAA, Pune (2021-2023), and I currently continue my research journey as a postdoctoral fellow at the Science Survey Center of the XMM-Newton project, based at the Observatoire Astronomique de Strasbourg, France.

Projects

My research interests include the study of accretion disks in compact objects, the investigation of relativistic jets and their relationship with the accretion disk, accretion geometry, quasi-periodic oscillations and their connection to accretion, corona, and disk instability. I have utilized observations from X-ray observatories such as AstroSat, NICER, Swift, NuSTAR, and IXPE for my research. Additionally, I have experience with various data analysis software, including HeaSoft, LAXPC software, SXT pipeline, NICERDAS, GHATS (General High-Energy Aperiodic Timing Software), and NuSTARDAS. Currently, I am contributing to updating the code for the Xcat-DB interface, which contains all the sources in the XMM archive sources catalog, extracted from more than 200 catalogs hosted primarily at CDS ( Strasbourg Astronomical Data Center).
